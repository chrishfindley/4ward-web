THREE NUMBERS.
EVERY COACH NEEDS TO KNOW.
This isn't new science. It's been in peer-reviewed journals for a decade. Nobody built a platform around it for high school programs. Until now.
1.7×
MORE LIKELY TO GET HURT.
Athletes sleeping under 8 hours are 1.7 times more likely to sustain a sports injury — controlling for sport, grade, and training volume. When poor sleep combines with a training load spike, that hazard ratio climbs to 2.25×. Your athletes don't tell you they slept 4 hours. The band does.
Milewski et al. — J. Pediatric Orthopaedics, 2014 · Von Rosen et al. — Scand. J. Med. Sci. Sports, 2017
25%
FEWER HIGH-INTENSITY SESSIONS. BETTER RESULTS.
Athletes in HRV-guided programs performed 25% fewer high-intensity sessions than teams on fixed programs — and outperformed them. Negative responders (athletes who got worse): 11% data-guided vs. 50% fixed. The athletes doing less hard work were improving more.
Vesterinen et al. — Med. Sci. Sports Exerc., 2016 · Manresa-Rocamora et al. — IJERPH, 2021
5%
FASTER. WITH ZERO EXTRA TRAINING.
Stanford varsity basketball players who extended sleep by two hours improved sprint speed 5%, free throw percentage 9%, and three-point shooting 9.2% — with no changes to training. None. Recovery is the adaptation. 4Ward makes it measurable for the first time at the high school level.
Mah et al. — Sleep Journal, 2011 (Stanford Sleep Lab)
